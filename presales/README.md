### Walk me through your discovery process with a new prospect.

**Employers ask this question to gauge how you uncover pain, map stakeholders, and qualify opportunities.** In your answer, outline a structured approach, mention frameworks or questions you rely on, and show how you tailor discovery for both technical and business audiences.

**Answer Example:** "I start by aligning on business outcomes, then dive into current workflows, constraints, and success metrics. ### How do you translate business requirements into a solution architecture the customer can trust?

**Employers ask this question to assess your ability to bridge business and technical contexts.** In your answer, describe how you move from outcomes to capabilities to architecture, and how you validate assumptions with the customer.

**Answer Example:** "I map each business requirement to a capability and then to specific product features or integrations, documenting assumptions openly. I'll whiteboard the data flows, identity model, and error paths, and highlight where we meet requirements natively vs. via API or partners. I check feasibility with engineering when needed and play it back to the customer for alignment. This builds confidence that the architecture is both sound and achievable."

## Document 3: Pre-Sales Understanding Questionnaire

### Client Engagement Key Phases

- **Pre-Sales** ↔ **Development & Delivery** ↔ **Post Sales**

### Customer Request Type

The customer is either new or existing. Customers ask for enhancements in their existing sold/developed solutions, buy your service, or ask to customize solutions.

- **New Customer (Pre-Sales)** - Workshop, Scope, Resource, Costing, timelines
- **Existing Customer (Pre-Sales)**
- **Enhancements (Demand)** - Assess, PoC, opportunities, solutions, testing
- **New assignment request (Opportunity-Pre-Sales)** - Workshop, Scope, Resource, Costing, timelines

Customer requests to a business or any of its internal organisations come in two forms – **opportunity** and **demand**.

An **opportunity** is a customer request for a new product, while a **demand** is a customer request to use or purchase an existing product provided by the business or its internal organisation. Here, a product means both a good and a service.

Before software development, pre-sale activities focus on engaging clients, understanding requirements, product demonstrations, needs assessment, presenting the solution and contract negotiation. Their primary goal is to demonstrate the value and benefits of the software to the client, ultimately leading to a successful sale.

Post-sale activities happen after software delivery, providing support, maintenance, and continuous improvement. Pre-sale includes needs assessment, solution presentation, proposal preparation, and contract negotiation. Post-sale provides implementation, training, support, maintenance, and relationship building.

### IT Consultancy vs Pre-Sales

**IT Consultancy:** IT consultancy involves providing expert advice and guidance to clients in information technology. IT consultants have specialized knowledge and experience in software, systems, and technology. They work closely with clients to understand their business objectives, challenges, and requirements. Based on their expertise, IT consultants provide recommendations, create strategies, and develop solutions to help clients optimize their IT infrastructure, systems, and processes. IT consultancy activities may include assessing current systems, designing IT architectures, providing technology roadmaps, offering implementation guidance, and supporting clients in achieving their technology goals.

While pre-sales focus on the sales process, emphasising presenting and promoting software solutions to potential clients, IT consultancy has a broader scope. IT consultants provide clients with strategic guidance and technical expertise, offering holistic solutions to address IT-related challenges and optimize technology investments. This outlines the key features, modules, and benefits of existing software solutions to customers.

It's important to note that pre-sales and IT consultancy often complement each other. Professionals in both roles collaborate closely to ensure that client requirements are understood, appropriate solutions are proposed, and successful software implementations are achieved.

Before software development, pre-sale activities focus on engaging clients, understanding requirements, and presenting solutions. Post-sale activities happen after software delivery, providing support, maintenance, and continuous improvement. Pre-sale includes needs assessment, solution presentation, proposal preparation, and contract negotiation. Post-sale provides implementation, training, support, maintenance, and relationship building.

### Pre-Sales Challenges

Software pre-sales IT challenges can be summarised as follows:

- Understanding customer requirements and pain points
- Balancing technical depth and simplicity in communication
- Addressing objections and overcoming resistance
- Customizing solutions to fit unique customer needs
- Managing the competitive landscape
- Handling technical concerns related to integration, security, scalability, and compatibility
- Managing stakeholders and decision-makers
- Adapting to changing technology and market trends

These challenges require effective communication, in-depth technical knowledge, customer-centricity, and navigating complex sales cycles. Overcoming these challenges is crucial for successful software pre-sales in the IT industry.
